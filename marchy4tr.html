The Sun Temple

Technical Report

MArch, Year 4 Design Resolution, University of Greenwich

A comprehensive technical study developed in alignment with the main design project The Sun and The Moon Temples. The report explores the integration of indigenous Muisca craftsmanship and symbolism with high-performance, sustainable architectural methods. Key focuses include:

- A glue-laminated timber (glulam) structural system, featuring gold inlays and inspired by traditional Muisca forms.

- Gilded aluminium cladding combined with bespoke symbolic ornaments crafted using indigenous techniques.

- Environmental strategies aligned with LETI’s Climate Emergency Design Guide.

- Passive solar design and material efficiency rooted in Muisca ecological knowledge.

- Specialist detailing and assembly techniques, including soldering, carving, and gilding.

- Spatial and formal orientation that reflects Muisca mythologies and cosmology.

This report demonstrates a holistic and culturally sensitive technical approach, where mythology, materiality, and climate- responsive design are deeply interwoven.
