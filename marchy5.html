David Deutsch's Experiment

The Many Worlds of David Deutsch

MArch, Year 5 Design Project, University of Greenwich

David Deutsch's Experiment is an architectural exploration deeply inspired by the Many Worlds Theory, championed by the renowned British physicist David Deutsch. This project envisions the profound intersection of quantum mechanics, consciousness, and architectural expression.

At the heart of this project lies Deutsch's ambition: constructing a quantum computer capable of connecting with his consciousness to observe firsthand the branching universes emerging from making decisions. The narrative unfolds simultaneously across two distinct but intertwined realities:

World I (Science):David Deutsch, the physicist, diligently works on his quantum computer, driven by the pursuit of concrete evidence of parallel universes.

World II (Art):Dawide Deutsch, the sculptor, passionately creates "The Soul of One," an artistic masterpiece that seeks to manifest the invisible realms of consciousness.

The project investigates the architectural fusion of these two worlds, capturing the intricate interactions at varying scales:

Microscale Interactions:

Subtle phenomena (mud, mold particles, faint sounds, and slight air currents) bridge the gap between worlds. These minute disturbances from David's scientific environment subtly penetrate Dawide's artistic space, creating nuanced inspirations that subtly shape his artistic creations.

Mesoscale Interactions:

At this intermediate stage, previously intangible interactions become conspicuous. Both David and Dawide begin perceiving strange phenomena (physical distortions, unexplained movements, and shifts in their surroundings) suggesting a tangible connection to an unseen counterpart. The architecture of their homes begins reflecting these heightened interactions, gradually mirroring and distorting elements from the parallel universe.

Macroscale Interactions:

The climax arrives as David integrates his consciousness with the quantum computer at the exact moment Dawide finishes "The Soul of One." This synchronization collapses the quantum barrier, allowing both realities to entangle. Their collective subconsciousness becomes entangled, marking a quantumly superposed state that initiates further branching, not from separation but from shared awareness.

The impact on architecture is profound. Both houses, once distinct, start reshaping and intertwining, reflecting a hybrid reality. The architectural forms echo, warp, and blend into one another, manifesting the entangled states physically in space. Landscapes and structures are redefined, presenting mirror-like realities bleeding into each other.

"David Deutsch's Experiment" reveals an ever-evolving, branching structure where art and science converge, inviting people to reflect deeply on the nature of reality, consciousness, and our interconnected multiverse.
